The plot above shows the cumulative price of Starbucks' stocks. This plot is misleading because it hides the changes in price and it makes it look like the price is always going up. The cumulative sum at time t will always be bigger than at t-1 (price is always positive). I decided to plot the cumulative sum for that reason

I think it's easy to create misleading data by presenting it in a slightly different way. The example I used to do, I manipulated the data to present in a way that makes mislead the reader into thinking the stock price for Starbucks is going up when that is not the case.
